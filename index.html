Things you should know before buying a car

Car Purchase Checklist

Buying your first car is exciting, but it can be overwhelming. A checklist helps keep you focused, ensures you ask the right questions, and reminds you to inspect key things like the tyres, service history, and take a test drive. With a clear plan, you gain confidence and avoid costly mistakes.

Choosing a Reliable Garage or Dealer

Before making a purchase, it's important to verify the credibility of the garage or car dealer. Look for reviews, ask for recommendations, and check how long they've been in business. Trustworthy sellers are transparent, patient, and open about warranties and return policies.

We’ve included a link below to help you find trusted dealers with verified information.

SIMI Advice for Buyers

SIMI (Society of the Irish Motor Industry) provides reliable and professional guidance for both new and used car buyers. Their resources help you spot scams, understand your rights, and make smart, informed choices.

Explore their website for expert tips, checklists, and a list of approved dealers.

Paying for Your Car – CCPC Guidance

The CCPC (Competition and Consumer Protection Commission) explains your payment options—whether you're paying upfront, financing with a loan, or using PCP or HP. They help you weigh the pros and cons so you can choose what fits your budget best.

Follow the link below to compare options and make an informed financial decision.

Understanding Motor Insurance

Before you drive, you’ll need the right insurance. The Citizen Information site covers everything: third-party vs. comprehensive cover, premium factors, post-accident steps, and how to make a claim.

Check out the link for a complete guide to motor insurance in Ireland.

Motor Tax Essentials

All car owners in Ireland must pay motor tax. The Citizen Information website explains tax rates, how to pay online, and what to do if your tax expires. It also includes info about tax exemptions and rules for electric vehicles.

Follow the link below to stay compliant and avoid penalties.

National Car Test (NCT)

The NCT ensures your vehicle is roadworthy. Most cars require testing every one or two years. The Citizen Information website outlines how to book, what to expect, costs, and next steps if your car fails the test.

Visit the link for everything you need to know about the NCT.

Resident Parking Permits – Galway City Council

If you live in Galway without private parking, you may qualify for a resident parking permit. This allows you to park for free in Pay & Display zones near your home. You’ll need to provide proof of address, car ownership, and insurance.

Download the application form and get full details using the link below.
