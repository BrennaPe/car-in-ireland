Car purchase checklist

Buying your first car? Super exciting—but easy to miss stuff if you’re not careful. That’s why a checklist is a game-changer. It keeps you on track, helps you ask the right questions, and makes sure you’re not forgetting things like checking the tyres, service history, or test driving. Trust me—when you’ve got a list, you feel way more confident, and it saves you from headaches later.

Check on the Garage or Car Dealer

Before buying, make sure you know who you’re dealing with. Is it a reliable garage or dealer? Always check reviews, ask around, and see how long they’ve been in business. A good seller will let you take your time, answer your questions, and be clear about things like warranty and returns.

SIMI Tips

When buying a new or used car, it’s super important to get your info from reliable places—and SIMI is one of the best. SIMI (Society of the Irish Motor Industry) gives clear, honest advice to help you make the right choice. They cover everything from spotting scams to understanding your rights as a buyer.

We’ve linked their website below so you can explore tips, checklists, and trusted dealers all in one place. If you’re serious about buying smart, start there!

Check Citizen Information for Extra Help

Buying a car isn’t just about picking the right one—it’s also about knowing your rights and responsibilities. That’s where Citizen Information comes in. Their website has loads of useful info about things like taxes, insurance, registration, and what to do if something goes wrong after you buy.

We’ve added the link below so you can check it out for yourself. It’s a great place to get clear answers, especially if you’re buying a car for the first time.

CCPC – Paying for Your Car

Figuring out how to pay for your car? The CCPC (Competition and Consumer Protection Commission) has great advice on your options—whether you’re thinking about paying upfront, using a loan, or going with PCP or HP finance. They explain the pros and cons of each one in simple terms, so you don’t get stuck in a deal that’s not right for you.

We’ve added the CCPC link below to help you compare and choose what works best for your budget. It’s all about making a smart, stress-free decision.

Motor Insurance – Citizen Information

Before you hit the road, you’ll need motor insurance—and the Citizen Information website explains everything you need to know.
